Poverty

Understanding the challenge is the first step towards solving it.

What is Poverty?

Poverty is not just about not having enough money. It is about a lack of basic human needs such as clean water, nutrition, healthcare, education, clothing, and shelter.


According to the World Bank, millions of people live on less than $2.15 a day. This is called "extreme poverty." It affects communities all over the world, limiting their ability to build a safe and stable future.

Causes of Poverty

Lack of Education

Without access to schools and skills training, people cannot find good-paying jobs to support their families.

Conflict & War

War destroys homes, businesses, and infrastructure, forcing people to leave everything behind and become refugees.

Climate Change

Droughts, floods, and storms destroy crops and homes, hitting the poorest farmers the hardest.

The Effects

  • Health: Poor nutrition leads to illness and stunted growth in children.
  • Economy: When people cannot work, the whole country's economy suffers.
  • Social Inequality: Poverty creates a gap between the rich and poor, leading to unfairness in society.

The cycle of poverty means that children born into poverty are likely to remain poor unless there is intervention.

Possible Solutions

Ending poverty requires action from governments, organizations, and individuals.


Key Actions:

  • Quality Education: Ensuring every child attends school.
  • Access to Healthcare: Basic medical care for everyone.
  • Clean Water: Building wells and sanitation systems.
  • Fair Wages: Paying workers enough to live a decent life.
